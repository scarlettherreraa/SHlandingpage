Early Life

Scarlett Herrera attended Saint Dominic School in Managua, Nicaragua. She had a highly successful high school career, graduating with a perfect 4.0 GPA and earning National Recognition of Academic Excellence.

Personal Life

Scarlett Herrera initially aspired to become a doctor, driven by a desire to help those in need. However, one day at school, she chose to stay in the computer lab during lunch to escape the heat outside. During this time, a professor introduced her to the basics of HTML. That very afternoon, she discovered a passion for coding and decided to pursue a career as a programmer.

Making decisions.

When it was time to apply for colleges, Scarlett found herself at a crossroads, needing to decide between becoming a programmer or a doctor. As she delved deeper into programming, she realized that she could still help people, albeit in a different way than as a doctor.

University Life

Scarlett decided to study Computer Science at FIU and has never regretted her choice to become a programmer. She has accomplished more than she ever imagined, met incredible people, worked with great teams, and even participated in a sprinternship at American Express.

Achievements and Celebrations.

Scarlett believes that every achievement, big or small, deserves to be celebrated. Below are some of her accomplishments.

Acceptance into FIU

In March 2022, Scarlett Herrera received her acceptance to FIU, and she was beyond excited to begin this new journey.

Highscool Graduation

In May 2022, Scarlett graduated as one of the top two students in her class and had the honor of delivering a speech to express her gratitude to the teachers and faculty of her school.

Beginning College

In Fall 2022, Scarlett enrolled in her first official coding class, taught by the exceptional Professor Christy Charters. Despite having minimal coding experience, Scarlett's background helped her perform well in the class and grasp the concepts effectively.

Break Through Tech Miami

In 2023, Scarlett dedicated herself to applying for internships, programs, and various opportunities. Through persistent effort, in 2024, she was accepted into the Break Through Tech (BTT) Miami Sprinternship program, founded by Judith Spitz and led at FIU by Nimmi Arunachalam. During the program, she prepared for a short internship (sprinternship) by spending seven weeks studying and practicing JavaScript, Python, and SQL.

Acceptance into Amex

Through the Break Through Tech program, Scarlett secured a position at American Express as a Mainframes Operations & Engineering Sprintern. Working alongside a fantastic team, she contributed to a project that developed a tool to filter through Amex's contract system. This experience sparked her passion for working with mainframes and databases, shaping her future career goals.
